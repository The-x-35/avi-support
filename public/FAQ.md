## Account

### The app is not loading or showing outdated info like old balances and missing cards. What should I do?

Give reinstalling the app a go! Make sure you download it from the App Store (not TestFlight) to ensure you are on the latest version. After reinstalling and logging back in, everything should load correctly. This fixes the issue most of the time.

### I switched to a new phone and now my Avici account looks empty. How do I get my account back?

No worries, your account is not gone. Just open Avici on your new phone and log in using the exact same method you used originally, whether that is Continue with Google, Continue with Apple, or Continue with Email. Using a different login method will show a blank account even with the same email address. Once you use the right method, everything should be right there waiting for you.

### I verified my identity but the app still shows my account as unverified. What is going on?

This is almost always a wrong login method situation. You probably completed verification on one account (say, Google login) but are currently logged in via Email or Apple, which is a separate account that has not been verified. Log out and sign back in using the method you originally used when you went through verification. Once you are in the right account, the verified status should show up correctly.

### I am not receiving the login verification code in my email. What should I do?

First, check your spam and promotions folders since it sometimes ends up there. If you still do not see it, the most common cause is accidentally unsubscribing from emails sent by Web3Auth, which is the service Avici uses for authentication. If you did unsubscribe at some point, that would block the code from arriving. Contact support and they can look into getting your email re-added to the subscription list.

### Can I change the email address on my Avici account?

Unfortunately, changing your login email is not something Avici is able to do right now. Your login email is directly tied to your wallet and your identity verification, so it cannot be swapped out. If you have lost access to your email, your best bet is to recover it through your email provider (Google, Apple, etc.) first, then log back into Avici. Avici can update your billing email address if needed, but the login email itself cannot be changed.

### I tried to verify my identity but it says my ID has already been used on another account. What does that mean?

This means you completed identity verification on a different Avici account at some point, probably without realizing it. Since you can only verify one account per identity, you cannot verify a second account with the same documents. The good news is your verified account is still out there. Try logging in with a different method (Google, Apple, or Email) and you should find the one that has your verification attached to it. Avici cannot transfer verification from one account to another.

### I logged in but my balance, card, and account details are all gone. What happened? *(common)*

This happens more often than you'd think, and it's almost always the same culprit: you logged in with a different method than the one you originally used. Avici has three separate login options (Continue with Google, Continue with Apple, and Continue with Email), and each one creates its own account even if the email address is the same. So if you signed up with Google but logged in with Email this time, you'd see a brand new empty account. Log out and try again with the correct method and your balance should reappear right away.

### How do I figure out which login method I used when I signed up? *(common)*

If you still have access to the app (even a blank-looking account), go to Settings in the top right corner, find your email address listed there, and tap it to copy it. That can help you identify the account. If you're totally locked out, reach out to support with any email addresses you may have used and they can check which one has an active verified account on file.

### I'm getting a login error or the app keeps sending me back to the login screen. How do I fix it? *(common)*

A few things tend to fix this. First, try switching your internet connection, swap between Wi-Fi and mobile data, or use a VPN. This often does the trick since the issue is usually a DNS or network problem rather than anything with your account. If that does not help, try reinstalling the app from the App Store (not TestFlight if you have both). Make sure you are on the latest version. Still stuck? Try opening the Web3Auth login page directly in your browser instead of letting it open in-app.

### How do I delete my Avici account? *(occasional)*

You can submit an account deletion request through the app. Once you do, the support team will process it. If you need written confirmation that your account has been closed and your balance is zero, just ask support after the deletion is complete and they can send that to you by email.

### The app is down or showing login issues for everyone. Is there a maintenance page I can check? *(occasional)*

Every now and then Avici goes through maintenance that can cause slower load times or login hiccups. When that happens, the team usually posts an update in the official Avici Telegram channel. You can check there for the latest status. If things seem to be down, just give it a little while and try again once the update goes out.

## Billing

### Can refunded money be transferred to my Avici Wallet?

Refunded funds can't be withdrawn to your Avici Wallet, unfortunately. They'll stay on your card and can be used for future card spending though!

### Why was I charged more than the listed price when buying something in a foreign currency?

Avici doesn't charge any transaction or forex fees, so that extra amount is most likely a cross-border fee from Visa. Visa typically charges around 0.4% to 1% on non-USD transactions, and exchange rates are set by Visa directly. Also, if you ever see a Dynamic Currency Conversion screen at checkout, make sure to select the local currency rather than USD to avoid extra charges.

### Why does my spending balance show a different amount than my total balance?

Your total balance is actually made up of two parts: your card balance and your wallet balance. The amount showing under 'spending balance' reflects just your card balance, not the combined total. So if the numbers look off, that's why!

### I got a refund but it's not showing in my balance. What's going on? *(common)*

No worries, this is totally normal! Refunds usually take 5 to 10 business days to show up in your app and card balance. The refund has to pass through the merchant's processor, then our processor, and finally into your account, so it takes a little time. In some cases, depending on the merchant's bank, it can take up to 31 days. If it still hasn't appeared after 31 days, contact the merchant and ask them for the RRN (Retrieval Reference Number), then share that with us and our card partners can trace it and get it sorted for you.

### Can I download a bank statement from Avici? *(common)*

You can download your card transaction statement directly from the app. Just tap the card icon at the bottom of your screen to open the Cards tab, and you'll see a small download icon next to 'Add Cards'. If you need a full formal bank statement, that's not available right now, but you can share your virtual account details as a PDF if that works for your purposes.

## Card

### My physical card keeps getting declined at stores and ATMs. How do I fix it?

A common reason for this is that your physical card PIN hasn't been set yet. Here's how to set it up:

1. Open the Avici app
2. Tap on your physical card
3. Find the PIN option and set your PIN

If you see 'Declined - Card Locked' in the transaction details, that means the card was locked and you'll need to contact support to get it unlocked. Once that's sorted and your PIN is set, try the card again at the merchant.

### I got a refund but my card balance hasn't been updated yet. Is something wrong?

Nothing to worry about here! Refunds typically take 5 to 10 business days to show up in your app. In some cases, depending on the merchant or their payment processor, it can take up to 31 days.

If you can already see the refund in your transaction list but it shows as 'Authorised', that just means it's still being processed. Once it flips to 'Completed', the amount will be added to your card balance.

If 31 days have passed and nothing has shown up, reach out to the merchant and ask for the RRN (Retrieval Reference Number). Share that with Avici support and they can trace the refund quickly.

### I tried to top up my card using SOL but nothing happened. How do I top up?

There are a couple of things that could be going on here. If you're topping up with SOL, you need to keep a minimum of $1.50 worth of SOL in your wallet for gas fees, so try topping up a slightly lower amount to account for that.

If it's still not working, the easiest fix is to swap your funds to USDC on Base or Polygon first, then top up from there. Avici covers the gas fees on those networks, so it tends to work more smoothly. Just keep in mind that gas fees on Solana and Ethereum are not covered, so those can sometimes cause issues.

### How long does it take to receive a physical card after ordering?

It depends on the shipping option you chose. If you paid for priority shipping (the $125 option includes this), your card should arrive within 1 to 2 weeks. Free standard shipping takes a bit longer, up to around 3 weeks depending on your region.

Tracking details will be sent to your registered email address once the card ships, and you should also be able to see it in the app around the same time.

### My card has disappeared from the app, what do I do? *(common)*

No worries, this is a known bug in the beta app and it's easy to fix! Here's what to do:

1. Log out of the Avici app
2. Clear the app from your recent apps
3. Log back in, making sure to use the correct login method (Google, Apple, or Email, whichever you signed up with)

Your card should be back right away. If you're logging in with Google, make sure you tap 'Continue with Google' specifically, since using a different login method, even with the same email, can show a completely different account.

### My card is not showing and the app is asking me to generate a new card. What's going on? *(common)*

This usually just means you're logged into the wrong account or the wrong login method. Avici has three ways to log in (Google, Apple, and Email) and each one can show a different account even if the email looks the same.

Try this:
1. Log out completely
2. Clear the app from recents
3. Log back in using the exact same method you used when you first signed up

If you're not sure which method you used, try 'Continue with Google' first since that's the most common one. If the card still doesn't appear after trying all three, reach out to support with your Avici email and they can check what's on your account.

### I need to update my shipping address for my physical card order. How do I do that? *(occasional)*

To update your shipping address, send an email from your Avici-registered email address to Growth@avici.club with the following info:

- The reason you need to update your address
- Your old address
- Your new updated address

This helps the team verify the request is coming from you. Once they confirm the update, you should receive tracking info to your registered email within a few days. Note that if your card has already been shipped, the address may not be updatable.

### Can I view my card details in the app? I tap the show button but nothing happens. *(occasional)*

To view your card details like the card number and CVV, you need to have biometric authentication (Face ID or fingerprint) set up on your device. The app uses this as a security check before revealing your card info.

Head into your phone's settings, enable Face ID or fingerprint unlock, and then try again in the app. It should work right after that. The team is also working on adding support for PIN or passcode as an alternative.

### Is the Avici card a credit card or debit card? Will it work for online verifications? *(occasional)*

The Avici card runs on a credit card BIN, so it behaves like a credit card even though you're loading your own funds onto it. It works at any merchant that accepts international Visa credit cards, which includes most major platforms, subscription services, and cloud providers like AWS, Google Cloud, and similar.

It also works internationally across most currencies. You can check the full details on the Avici docs at docs.avici.money/getting-started/secured-credit-cards.

### Can I withdraw cash from an ATM using a virtual card? *(occasional)*

Yes, but only if the ATM supports NFC (contactless/tap) payments for international Visa credit cards. This varies a lot by region and not all ATMs support it. For example, NFC ATM withdrawals are not very common in Bangladesh yet.

For physical cards, you can withdraw from ATMs that accept chip or swipe as well, not just tap. The daily ATM withdrawal limit is $250. You can find the full fees and limits at docs.avici.money/getting-started/secured-credit-cards/fees-and-limits.

### Does the Visa Airport Companion benefit come with the Platinum card? *(occasional)*

The Visa Airport Companion benefit is only available on the Signature card, not the Platinum card. If airport lounge access is important to you, you'll want to go with the Signature.

### A merchant keeps trying to charge me even after I cancelled my subscription. Can you stop the payments? *(occasional)*

Unfortunately Avici doesn't have control over active subscription charges from merchants. Your best move is to contact the merchant directly to cancel the subscription on their end.

In the meantime, you can temporarily freeze your card from within the app to block any further charges while you sort it out with the merchant.

### A transaction I made is showing as declined because the merchant is blocked. Is my account okay? *(occasional)*

Your account is totally fine! Sometimes a specific merchant gets flagged by the card issuer's risk monitoring system, which causes all transactions to that merchant to be declined as a precaution. This is to protect your funds and has nothing to do with your account or card status.

Unfortunately this isn't something Avici can override on their end. There's no set timeline for when the investigation wraps up, but once it does, transactions to that merchant should work again.

### I'm not receiving the OTP email needed to verify my card transaction. What should I do? *(occasional)*

Check your spam or junk folder! OTP verification emails sometimes end up there instead of your main inbox. Once you find it, you should be good to go.

## Funds

### I want to send funds from my Avici wallet to a EUR bank account (SEPA withdrawal) but I can't see any tokens to send. What should I do?

The virtual account currently only supports USDC on the Solana network for withdrawals to your EUR IBAN. If your USDC is sitting on Base or Mantle, you'll need to swap it to USDC on Solana first. Just tap Swap under your total balance, select your USDC, and choose USDC on Solana as the destination. Once the swap is done, you should be able to proceed with your bank withdrawal.

### A transfer I received got returned or rejected. How long will it take for the sender to get their money back?

If a transfer gets rejected (for example, because your account doesn't support third-party payments), it gets automatically sent back to the sender's bank. The timeline can vary depending on the sender's bank, but ask the sender to check their account and also follow up with their bank directly if they haven't received it after a few business days. If it's been a while and still nothing, reach out to Avici support with a transaction receipt and they can escalate it with their banking partners.

### My app is showing the wrong balance or things like my cards aren't showing up. How do I fix this?

This is usually a sync or connection issue and it's pretty easy to fix. Try logging out and back in first, that resolves it most of the time. If that doesn't do it, try switching your internet connection (Wi-Fi to mobile data or vice versa), then log in again. As a last resort, uninstall the app, reinstall it, and log back in. Most users find their balance and cards show up correctly after one of these steps!

### Is there a USD bank account available, or do I only get a EUR IBAN?

Right now, whether you get a USD or EUR virtual account depends on your region and which banking partner covers your area. Not all regions currently have USD banking available. Avici is working on migrating to a new banking partner which should expand USD availability to more regions soon. To check what's available for you, open your Virtual Account tab and tap the Info icon to see your account's capabilities.

### Card refunds are taking a while to show up. Is that normal?

Totally normal! Refunds typically take 5 to 10 business days to appear in the app. The money has to travel from the merchant's processor to Avici's processor and then into your account, so it's a multi-step journey. If it hasn't shown up after 10 business days, that's a good time to reach out to support.

### I sent a SEPA/bank transfer to my Avici account but it hasn't arrived yet. What's going on? *(common)*

No worries, this happens sometimes! SEPA transfers are usually instant but can take up to 24 hours depending on the bank, payment rail, and whether it was sent over a weekend. If your transfer is still missing after 24 hours, reach out to support with your account email and a screenshot of the transaction receipt so the team can escalate it with their banking partners for you.

### My deposit shows as confirmed on-chain but I can't see it in the app. Where did my funds go? *(common)*

This is usually just a sync issue with the app. Try these steps and it should sort itself out: restart the app, then check your balance again. If that doesn't work, switch your internet connection (for example, swap from Wi-Fi to mobile data or try a VPN). Still not showing? Uninstall the app, reinstall it, log back in, and check again. A few users found their balance was showing correctly after a fresh install!

### Can my Avici virtual account receive payments from someone else's bank account (third-party transfers)? *(common)*

It depends on your account's capabilities. Some accounts are set up for first-party SEPA only, which means only transfers from a bank account in your own name will be accepted. If a third-party payment comes in and your account doesn't support it, the money gets automatically sent back to the sender. You can check your account's capabilities by going to the Virtual Account tab, tapping the Info icon in the top right, and looking under Capabilities. Third-party support may become available in more regions soon as Avici migrates to a new banking partner.

### I got a refund from a merchant but I can't withdraw it from my card balance to crypto. Why? *(common)*

Merchant refunds land in your card spending balance, and unfortunately that balance can't be moved back to your Avici crypto wallet. You can still use it for card purchases or ATM withdrawals though! Only funds that weren't originally refunds can be withdrawn to the wallet. It's a bit of a limitation right now, so the best bet is to spend the refunded amount via card or take it out at an ATM.

### I'm getting high slippage when swapping or topping up my card. Is there a cheaper way to do it? *(common)*

The culprit is usually the Mantle network. Avici doesn't have a card contract on Mantle, so any top-up from there has to be routed through a swap first, which causes significant slippage. The fix is to swap your USDC to Base or Polygon instead, since Avici has card contracts on both those networks and the slippage is much lower (usually under $0.50). You can do this by tapping Swap under your total balance, selecting your current asset, and choosing USDC on Base or Polygon as the destination.

### I'm trying to top up my card on Solana but it's not going through. Do I need SOL for gas? *(common)*

Yep, Solana transactions do need a little SOL to cover gas fees. Avici sponsors gas fees for up to 5 Solana transactions per day, but once you hit that limit (or for card top-ups specifically) you'll need at least $1.50 worth of SOL in your wallet to cover the fee. If you've hit your daily limit, you can either add some SOL or just wait 24 hours for the free transaction allowance to reset. Alternatively, you can use an EVM network like Base or Polygon for top-ups instead and avoid this entirely.

## KYC

### I already verified on one Avici account and now I'm trying to verify on a new account. Why won't it work?

Avici only allows one verification per person. So if you already completed KYC on a previous account (even if you deleted it), you can't use the same identity documents to verify a second account. Your best bet is to go back to your original verified account if it still exists. If you deleted it, reach out to support with your old email address and they can check whether anything can be done.

### The KYC camera won't open or the verification screen is frozen during the process. How do I fix it?

Good news, this one usually has a simple fix! Here's what to check:

- Make sure the app has camera permission enabled on your phone
- If the camera icon appears in red during verification, tap it and allow access
- Disable any VPN you have running before retrying
- If you're on iOS, turn off iCloud Private Relay (this is a big one, it breaks the camera for a lot of people)
- Try opening the KYC link directly in Safari instead of inside the app

Once you've done those, restart the KYC process and it should go through.

### My proof of address was rejected. What documents are actually accepted?

No worries, here's what's accepted for proof of address:

- Utility bills
- Bank or credit card statements
- Tax invoices
- Government-issued residential statements or certificates

Just make sure whatever you submit is dated within the last 3 months, otherwise it'll get rejected. Rental agreements unfortunately aren't on the accepted list, so if that's what you tried, you'll need one of the above instead.

### Is Avici available in my country? Why isn't my country showing up in the verification?

Not all countries and regions are supported yet, which is why some people hit a wall during verification. For Avici cards, India is currently unavailable (the team is working on it). For virtual accounts, there's also a list of supported and prohibited regions. You can check the full lists here: https://docs.avici.money/getting-started/secured-credit-cards/regions-and-prohibitions-for-avici-card and https://docs.avici.money/getting-started/virtual-accounts/available-and-prohibited-regions. If your country isn't supported for one product, you might still be able to use the other, so it's worth checking both lists.

### My verification got stuck because I closed or minimized the app during the KYC process. Can it be reset?

Yes, it can! If you closed or minimized the app mid-verification, it can get stuck on the 'Verifying your status' screen with no way to continue. The support team can escalate this to get your verification reset, which gives you a fresh link to start over. Just be aware it may take a few days. If you want a faster fix, you can also create a new account with a different email address and complete verification there instead. Either way, make sure camera permissions are enabled and any VPN or iCloud Private Relay is turned off before you try again.

### My KYC verification is stuck on 'Verifying your status' and won't move. What do I do? *(common)*

This happens more often than you'd think! Here are a few things to try in order:

1. Log out of the app and log back in
2. If that doesn't help, try reinstalling the app
3. Make sure you're using the correct login method (Google, Apple, or email) that you originally signed up with

If it's still stuck after all that, reach out to support through the app (Settings > Customer Support) and the team can escalate it to reset your verification so you can start fresh. Just be aware that if it needs a manual reset it can take a few business days.

### How long does KYC verification usually take? *(common)*

Usually it's instant! But sometimes it can get delayed depending on the documents you submitted. If it's been a while, here's what to expect: card verification can take 5 to 7 business days or more in some cases, and business account verification is typically 3 to 5 business days. Delays can happen if a document looks outdated or if the biometric check needs a closer look. If you've been waiting longer than that, it's worth reaching out to support so they can check on it.

### My KYC verification failed or was declined. Why, and can I try again? *(common)*

Sorry to hear that! A few things could cause this. The most common reasons are: submitting documents from an unsupported country or region, using the same documents on more than one account, or the documents not passing the automated checks by Avici's card partner (Rain). Unfortunately, when a verification is declined by the card partner, that decision is final and can't be appealed or resubmitted. If you're not sure why it failed, you can contact support and they'll look into it, but do keep in mind they may not always have the exact reason since it's handled by the partner.

### My account holder name is wrong after verification. Can it be corrected? *(occasional)*

Yes, this can be fixed! Just reach out to support through the app and share your Avici account email. The team will escalate it to the relevant partner to get the name updated. Once it's done, restart the app and the correct name should show up. Logging out and back in alone might not be enough, so a full app restart is the way to go.

### The Sumsub verification link I received has expired. What should I do? *(occasional)*

These links do expire if you wait too long to use them. Just contact support and ask for a new verification link. They can send you a fresh one so you can complete the process. Once you have it, try to complete it in one go without closing or minimizing the app.

## Wallet

### Why can't I do transactions on Ethereum even though I have ETH in my wallet?

This trips a lot of people up! The key thing to know is that for Ethereum transactions, you need native ETH on the Ethereum network itself. If your ETH is sitting on a different network like Optimism, it won't cover gas fees on Ethereum. Also, Avici does not cover gas fees on Ethereum, so you need to have enough native ETH on the right network to complete any transaction. Just add some native ETH to the Ethereum network and you should be good to go.

### Which networks does Avici cover gas fees for?

Good question! Avici covers gas fees for most supported networks, but there are a couple of exceptions to keep in mind. Gas fees on Ethereum are not covered, so you will need native ETH on the Ethereum network for those transactions. On Solana, gas-free swaps and withdrawals are limited to 5 per day. For all other supported networks, you are all good and Avici has the gas covered.

### Are there any swap fees on Avici?

Great news, there are no swap fees on Avici! The one thing to keep in mind is gas fees. Avici does not sponsor gas fees for Ethereum, so if you are swapping on ETH you will need to cover that yourself. For networks like Arbitrum though, there are no gas fees for swapping either. Topping up your card also has no gas fee.

### I'm having trouble with swaps or withdrawals after logging in on a new phone. Is that normal?

This can happen if you are logging in from a different device than the one you originally created your account on. Try switching back to the original phone or device you used to set up your Avici account and see if that resolves it. If you are still having issues after that, reach out and we can look into it further with you.

### I transferred crypto to my Avici wallet but it's not showing up in my balance. What should I do? *(common)*

No worries, this is usually a quick fix! Try doing a hard restart of the app: log out, fully close the app by removing it from your recent apps, then log back in. That refreshes everything and your balance should show up correctly. If it still looks off after that, reach out and we can dig deeper.

### My wallet balance or recent transactions aren't showing correctly. How do I fix this? *(common)*

This one is usually solved with a quick app refresh! Here is what to do: log out of the app, clear it from your recent apps, then log back in. Make sure you are on the latest version of the app too. Most of the time everything shows up correctly after that. If it keeps happening, just let us know!

### I have a refund showing as spending balance but I can't withdraw it. Why not? *(occasional)*

Refunded amounts unfortunately cannot be withdrawn back to your Avici wallet. The good news is you can absolutely use that balance for card spending, so it is not lost! Just use it next time you make a purchase with your card.

## General

### What are the ATM withdrawal limits for my Avici card?

You can withdraw up to $250 per day, with a max of 3 ATM transactions per card per day (this includes balance checks and declined attempts). You can see the full breakdown of fees and limits here: https://docs.avici.money/getting-started/secured-credit-cards/fees-and-limits

### Are there limits on how much I can receive or send via my virtual account?

There is no maximum limit on how much you can receive into your virtual account. For sending, the standard annual limit is $27,000, but you can unlock higher or unlimited limits by completing Tier 2 verification. For more details, tap the "i" icon in the top right corner of your virtual account screen, or check the docs here: https://docs.avici.money/getting-started/virtual-accounts/fees-and-limits

### Can I send money from Avici directly to someone else's bank account?

Yes, you can! Tap Withdraw, add a payee with their bank details, and the USDC from your Avici wallet gets converted and sent to their bank in USD or EUR. It works for both US bank transfers (ACH) and SEPA for Euro accounts. Just make sure you are holding USDC on the Solana network, since that is what the virtual accounts currently support.

### How do I open a virtual bank account on Avici?

Easy! Tap "Add Money" on the home screen, select "Cash" at the bottom, then tap "Open Your Account" and follow the verification steps. Once it is set up, you will see your USD and EUR account details. If the button loads and then resets without doing anything, just close and try again and it should go through.

### Does Avici support Bitcoin? Can I send BTC to my wallet?

Native BTC is not supported right now, but you can send Wrapped BTC (WBTC) on a supported network. Solana is the recommended option since you can export your private key there if an asset ends up unsupported on the Avici Wallet.

### What is the difference between the Visa Platinum and Visa Signature cards?

Both are great options, but there are some differences in perks and limits between the two. You can compare them side by side here: https://docs.avici.money/getting-started/secured-credit-cards/virtual-and-physical-cards

### Are physical cards available, and how much do they cost?

Yes, Avici offers both Visa Platinum and Visa Signature physical cards. You can find all the details including costs here: https://docs.avici.money/getting-started/secured-credit-cards/virtual-and-physical-cards

### Does Avici support 3D Secure or OTP for transactions?

Yes, 3D Secure is already enabled on your Avici card. When you shop at a merchant that requires it, you will automatically get an OTP sent to your billing email or phone number. To check or update your billing contact details, go to the Cards tab, select your card, and tap "Billing".

### I want to apply for a job at Avici. How do I do that?

You can fill out the application form here: https://forms.avici.money/jointeam. If your profile is a good fit for an open role, the hiring team will reach out to you directly. Support agents are not able to provide updates on application status, so the form is your best bet!

### I am seeing fake Avici support accounts or scammers in Telegram groups. What should I do?

Unfortunately scammers do create fake Telegram groups, and they can add you without permission if your settings allow it. To protect yourself, go to Settings in Telegram, then Privacy and Security, then Groups and Channels, and switch it to "My Contacts" so strangers cannot add you to groups. The only official Avici support channels are the in-app support, this Telegram, and the official X account. Avici will never have a support link on a random website.

### What is the difference between a personal and a business account on Avici?

Business accounts come with increased transaction limits and specialised business support. Avici also offers Business Cards and dedicated onramp and offramp options for business users. You can read more here: https://docs.avici.money/getting-started/business-cards/what-are-business-cards#business-vs-personal-cards, or reach out to the Biz team directly at https://t.me/avicibiz for a more detailed conversation.

### Why doesn't Avici offer cashback on card transactions? *(occasional)*

It comes down to how Avici structures its fees. Rather than charging you fees and giving some back as cashback, Avici keeps the fees as low as possible to begin with. Think 0% FX markup, 0% swap fees, 0% on-ramp fees, and no hidden charges like low-authorization or decline fees. Many cashback products end up net negative for users once you factor in all those underlying fees.
